Topology: Use a passive twin-T notch filter referenced to ground. This is the most direct easy band-stop realization with only primitive `R` and `C` elements and no active devices or supply rails. Drive the filter with an independent AC source named `V1` connected as `V1 Vin 0 AC 1`. Observe the filtered response at `Vout`.

Required devices:
- 1 independent AC voltage source: `V1 Vin 0 AC 1`
- 3 resistors for the resistive T branch
- 3 capacitors for the capacitive T branch
- Optional very large load resistor from `Vout` to ground only if needed for numerical stability; keep it large enough not to disturb the notch

Key interconnectivity:
- Build two T networks in parallel between `Vin` and `Vout`.
- Resistive T branch:
  - Two equal resistors in series from `Vin` to `Vout`
  - One capacitor from the midpoint of those two resistors to ground
- Capacitive T branch:
  - Two equal capacitors in series from `Vin` to `Vout`
  - One resistor from the midpoint of those two capacitors to ground
- Use standard twin-T notch ratios for a deep null:
  - Series resistors: `R`, `R`
  - Shunt resistor: `R/2`
  - Series capacitors: `C`, `C`
  - Shunt capacitor: `2C`
