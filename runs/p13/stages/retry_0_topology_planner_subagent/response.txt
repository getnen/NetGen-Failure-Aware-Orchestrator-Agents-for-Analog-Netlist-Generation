**Topology Summary**  
Use a passive twin-T notch filter between `Vin` and `Vout`, with the two T networks connected in parallel and both referenced to ground. One branch is a resistive T with a shunt capacitor; the other is a capacitive T with a shunt resistor. With standard twin-T ratios, the notch is centered at `f0 = 1 / (2*pi*R*C)`, nominally about 159 kHz for `R = 1k` and `C = 1n`.

**Device/Function List**  
- `V1 Vin 0 AC 1`: independent AC drive source
- Resistive T branch:
  - `R` from `Vin` to resistive midpoint
  - `R` from resistive midpoint to `Vout`
  - `2C` capacitor from resistive midpoint to ground
- Capacitive T branch:
  - `C` from `Vin` to capacitive midpoint
  - `C` from capacitive midpoint to `Vout`
  - `R/2` resistor from capacitive midpoint to ground
- Optional: one very large resistor from `Vout` to ground only if the simulator needs a DC reference at the output

**Bias/Reference Strategy**  
No supply rails or active-device biasing are needed. Node `0` is the only reference. `Vin` is driven directly by `V1`, so it is not floating. `Vout` is the passive observation node and must remain exactly named `Vout`.

**Signal Or Feedback Path**  
The signal reaches `Vout` through two passive paths in parallel. Near the notch frequency, the phase/amplitude contributions of the resistive-T and capacitive-T branches cancel at `Vout`, creating the stop-band null. There is no active feedback loop; cancellation is purely from passive network superposition.

**SPICE/Netlist Constraints**  
- Final file: `p13.cir`
- Exact ports: `Vin`, `Vout`
- Use only primitive `V`, `R`, `C`
- Include exactly: `.ac dec 20 1 1e9`
- Keep twin-T ratios exact for a deep notch:
  - series resistors = `R`, `R`
  - shunt resistor = `R/2`
  - series capacitors = `C`, `C`
  - shunt capacitor = `2C`

**Failure Risks To Avoid**  
- Do not swap the shunt elements between the two T branches
- Do not break the parallel connection between `Vin` and `Vout`; both T networks must span the same endpoints
- Do not rename or omit `Vin` or `Vout`
- Do not add active devices, dependent sources, or supply rails
- Avoid inaccurate ratios or mismatched “equal” parts; notch depth is highly sensitive to matching
- If adding a load/stability resistor at `Vout`, keep it very large so it does not materially spoil the notch or insertion loss
