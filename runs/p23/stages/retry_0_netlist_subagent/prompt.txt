=== TOPOLOGY PLAN ===
**Topology Summary**

Plan `p23.cir` as a 5 V single-supply transistor-level Wien Bridge oscillator with output node exactly named `Vout`. Use one compact analog gain core configured as a non-inverting amplifier around a mid-supply reference. The oscillator loop is the classic Wien bridge from `Vout` to `Vinp`, with negative feedback from `Vout` to `Vinn`.

Use a real transistor-level op-amp-like stage, preferably:
- NMOS differential input pair with inputs `Vinp` and `Vinn`
- PMOS current-mirror or active-load devices
- NMOS tail current source biased from a simple resistor/MOS bias branch
- A modest common-source voltage gain/output stage driving `Vout`
- Optional light output resistance or small load capacitor only for convergence

No behavioral amplifier, controlled-source core, ideal op-amp macro, or hard rail-switching inverter should be used.

**Device/Function List**

- `VDD`: independent 5 V supply from `VDD` to `0`
- Mid-supply divider:
  - resistor from `VDD` to `Vref`
  - equal resistor from `Vref` to `0`
  - bypass capacitor from `Vref` to `0`
- Gain core:
  - transistor differential pair using explicit nodes `Vinp` and `Vinn`
  - active load/current mirror for voltage gain
  - output stage connected to `Vout`
  - bias devices/resistors for tail current and output-stage operating point
- Wien bridge:
  - `Rw1` and `Cw1` in series from `Vout` to `Vinp`
  - `Rw2` and `Cw2` in parallel from `Vinp` to `Vref`
  - use matched values, e.g. `10k` and `10nF`
- Negative feedback:
  - `Rf` from `Vout` to `Vinn`
  - `Rg` from `Vinn` to `Vref`
  - use gain slightly above 3, e.g. `Rf=22k`, `Rg=10k`
- Optional gentle amplitude stabilization:
  - only if needed, use soft transistor/diode degeneration or mild nonlinear feedback
  - avoid output clamp diodes directly on `Vout`

**Bias/Reference Strategy**

Create `Vref` at approximately 2.5 V using the divider and bypass capacitor. Bias both `Vinp` and `Vinn` near `Vref` at DC. The transistor gain stage must also bias `Vout` near mid-supply so the sinusoid can swing both upward and downward without immediately clipping.

The gain stage must provide enough open-loop gain that the closed-loop non-inverting gain is slightly greater than 3 at startup. Since the Wien network attenuates by about 1/3 at resonance, the loop gain must initially exceed unity.

Include startup asymmetry with `.ic`, for example `Vinp` slightly above `Vref` and/or `Vinn` slightly below `Vref`. There are no external input nodes.

**Signal Or Feedback Path**

Positive frequency-selective path:

`Vout -> series Rw1/Cw1 -> Vinp`, with `Vinp -> parallel Rw2/Cw2 -> Vref`

At approximately:

`f0 = 1 / (2*pi*Rw*Cw)`

with `Rw=10k` and `Cw=10nF`, expect about `1.59 kHz`.

Negative gain-setting path:

`Vout -> Rf -> Vinn -> Rg -> Vref`

This sets the non-inverting closed-loop gain near:

`1 + Rf/Rg = 3.2`

which is slightly above the Wien startup requirement.

**SPICE/Netlist Constraints**

- Preserve node names `Vout`, `Vinp`, `Vinn`, `Vref`, `VDD`, and `0`
- Use primitive MOS/BJT/resistor/capacitor/diode devices only for the active gain stage
- Do not use `E`, `F`, `G`, `H`, behavioral sources, ideal limiters, or op-amp subcircuit macros
- Required analysis: `.tran 1u 5m`
- MOS bulk convention: NMOS bulks to their sources or lowest local potential; PMOS bulks to their sources or highest local potential
- Keep the bridge centered around `Vref`, not ground
- Keep any convergence capacitor at `Vout` small enough not to suppress oscillation

**Failure Risks To Avoid**

- Too little gain: oscillation decays to DC near `Vref`
- Too much gain or hard limiting: `Vout` clips flat at 0 V or 5 V
- Referencing the Wien shunt branch to ground instead of `Vref`: wrong DC bias and likely failed startup
- Omitting explicit `Vinp`/`Vinn`: startup initialization becomes difficult
- Using an inverter-like CMOS stage as the main oscillator core: produces rail switching instead of a sinusoid
- Adding extra output buffers or multiple feedback ladders: creates unwanted poles and competing loop behavior
- Oversized output/load capacitance: shifts phase and can suppress the Wien oscillation
- Direct clamp diodes on `Vout`: distort the sine wave and violate the clean Wien bridge intent
