**Topology Summary**
Use one NMOS as a low-side constant current sink with a resistive pull-up load. `Vout` is the shared drain/load node. The resistor connects from `VDD` to `Vout`; the NMOS connects from `Vout` to ground. The gate is driven directly by `Vbias`.

**Device/Function List**
- `VDD`: DC supply source from `VDD` to `0`, value `5 V`.
- `Vbias`: DC bias source named explicitly as `Vbias`, connected `Vbias 0`, value `DC 1.0`.
- `R`: load resistor from `VDD` to `Vout`, nominal `10k`.
- NMOS transistor: drain=`Vout`, gate=`Vbias`, source=`0`, bulk=`0`.
- `.model NMOS NMOS`: threshold around `0.5-0.7 V`, `kp=100u`.
- NMOS sizing: nominal `W=50u`, `L=1u`.

**Bias/Reference Strategy**
`VDD=5 V` establishes the pull-up rail. `Vbias=1.0 V` gives the NMOS `Vgs=1.0 V`, above threshold, so the transistor sinks current from `Vout` through its channel to ground. The resistor converts that sink current into a DC output voltage at `Vout`.

**Signal or Feedback Path**
There is no active feedback or mirror reference branch. The DC path is:
`VDD -> R -> Vout -> NMOS drain/source -> 0`.

`Vbias` only controls the NMOS gate and should not be loaded except by the MOS gate.

**SPICE/Netlist Constraints**
- Preserve node names exactly: `Vbias`, `Vout`.
- `Vout` must be the NMOS drain and the lower terminal of the resistor.
- `Vbias` must be electrically driven by an explicit independent DC source.
- Include `.op` analysis.
- Include an `NMOS` model named exactly `NMOS`.
- Do not write a diode-connected NMOS, reference leg, or full current mirror.

**Failure Risks To Avoid**
- Do not leave `Vbias` floating or rename it to something like `VB`.
- Do not connect the load resistor to ground; it must connect from `VDD` to `Vout`.
- Do not swap NMOS drain/source in a way that makes `Vout` the source.
- Do not omit the bulk connection to ground.
- Avoid choosing `Vbias` below threshold, which would turn the sink mostly off.
- Ensure resistor/device sizing does not force `Vout` near ground so hard that the NMOS leaves saturation unexpectedly.
