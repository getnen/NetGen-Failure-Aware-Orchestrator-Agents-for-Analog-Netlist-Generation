Objective: Create a 5 V single-supply RC phase-shift oscillator whose output node is exactly `Vout`. The circuit must produce a visible sustained periodic waveform at `Vout` during `.tran 1u 5m`. There are no external input nodes.

Topology: Use an op-amp-like high-gain inverting amplifier biased at mid-supply, followed by a three-section RC phase-shift feedback ladder from `Vout` back to the amplifier inverting input. The RC ladder should provide approximately 180 degrees phase shift at the oscillation frequency, while the inverting gain stage provides the other 180 degrees. The amplifier gain magnitude must exceed 29 at startup so oscillation grows from a small imbalance, then be limited by output swing or nonlinear gain behavior so the waveform does not remain at tiny amplitude.

Required devices:
- 5 V supply source from `VDD` to `0`.
- Mid-supply reference generator at `Vcm`, preferably two equal resistors from `VDD` to `0` with a bypass capacitor from `Vcm` to `0`.
- Behavioral or transistor/op-amp gain stage with explicit internal differential input nodes, such as `Vinp` and `Vinn`.
- The non-inverting input `Vinp` biased to `Vcm`.
- The inverting input `Vinn` driven only by the RC feedback ladder and its bias/gain-setting resistor network.
- Three RC ladder sections between `Vout` and `Vinn`, using nodes such as `n1`, `n2`, and `n3`.
- Feedback/gain-setting resistors arranged so the inverting amplifier small-signal gain is comfortably above 29, for example about 35 to 50.
- Output limiting compatible with 0 V to 5 V supply rails so `Vout` swings around `Vcm` rather than saturating permanently.

Key interconnectivity:
- `VDD` is an independent DC source of 5 V.
- `Vcm` is generated as 2.5 V.
- `Vinp` connects to `Vcm`, with an initial condition or tiny offset allowed for startup.
- `Vout` feeds the RC phase-shift ladder: `Vout -> C/R section 1 -> n1 -> C/R section 2 -> n2 -> C/R section 3 -> n3/Vinn`.
- The ladder should be referenced to `Vcm`, not directly to ground, so the oscillator works correctly on a single 5 V supply.
- Use equal RC sections for predictable oscillation. A practical choice is R = 10 kOhm and C = 10 nF, giving oscillation in the low kHz range, well visible over 5 ms.
- Add a large bias resistor from `Vinn` or the final ladder node to `Vcm` if needed to define DC operating point without loading the ladder excessively.
- The gain stage output must be exactly node `Vout`.

Biasing and startup assumptions:
- Single-supply operation requires all small-signal nodes to sit near `Vcm = 2.5 V` at DC.
- If MOSFETs are used, connect each MOS bulk to its source and bias gates threshold-aware using NMOS Vth = 0.7 V and PMOS Vth = -0.7 V.
- If using a behavioral op-amp, explicitly include supply-limited output behavior and high open-loop gain.
- Add startup asymmetry using `.ic` on internal nodes, for example `V(Vinp)=2.51`, `V(Vinn)=2.49`, or slight initial capacitor voltages across the RC ladder. The checker expects startup from a slight internal input mismatch and then sustained oscillation at `Vout`.
- Avoid making the amplifier gain too low; the previous attempt had only about 20 mV output amplitude, so increase loop gain and ensure the feedback network is not over-damped.

Required analysis:
- Include exactly the requested transient analysis: `.tran 1u 5m`.
- Use `.ic` or startup-compatible initial conditions so oscillation begins without relying on numerical noise alone.
- No external input source is required because the task has no input nodes.

Expected behavior and output constraints:
- `Vout` must appear in the final netlist and must be the oscillator output.
- During transient analysis, `Vout` should grow from the initial mismatch and settle into a sustained approximately sinusoidal or mildly clipped periodic waveform centered near 2.5 V.
- The latter half of the 5 ms transient should show clear nonzero oscillation amplitude, preferably hundreds of millivolts to several volts peak-to-peak, not the 20 mV-level failed behavior.
- Frequency should be consistent with the RC phase-shift network; for R = 10 kOhm and C = 10 nF, expect oscillation on the order of 600 Hz to 1 kHz depending on amplifier loading and gain implementation.
- Do not leave any named node floating.
