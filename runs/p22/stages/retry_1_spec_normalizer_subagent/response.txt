Objective: Create a 5 V single-supply RC phase-shift oscillator whose output node `Vout` develops a sustained visible periodic waveform during `.tran 1u 5m`. There are no external input nodes. `Vout` must appear as the oscillator output node and must not remain near DC; target late-time oscillation amplitude should be clearly larger than the failed 20 mV result.

Topology: Use an op-amp-style high-gain inverting stage biased around a mid-supply reference, with a three-section RC phase-shift feedback ladder from `Vout` back to the inverting input. The RC ladder supplies approximately 180 degrees phase shift at the oscillation frequency, and the inverting gain stage supplies the additional 180 degrees. Set closed-loop/inverting gain magnitude above the Barkhausen minimum of 29, preferably around 35 to 45, so startup is reliable.

Required devices and interconnectivity:
- Independent DC supply `VDD` = 5 V from node `VDD` to ground.
- Mid-supply reference node, e.g. `Vref`, generated by a resistor divider from `VDD` to ground and bypassed with a capacitor. `Vref` should be near 2.5 V.
- Op-amp/gain-stage internal input nodes should include `Vinp` and `Vinn` if using a behavioral op-amp. Tie `Vinp` to `Vref`. Drive `Vinn` from the RC feedback network plus bias/gain resistors.
- Output of the gain stage is exactly `Vout`.
- Three cascaded RC high-pass phase-shift sections from `Vout` to `Vinn`, for example:
  - `Vout` through `C1` to node `n1`, with `R1` from `n1` to `Vref`
  - `n1` through `C2` to node `n2`, with `R2` from `n2` to `Vref`
  - `n2` through `C3` to feedback node `n3`, with `R3` from `n3` to `Vref`
  - connect `n3` to `Vinn` through the inverting input/gain network as needed
- Choose equal RC sections such as `R1=R2=R3=10k` and `C1=C2=C3=10nF`, giving oscillation in the low-kHz range, suitable for a 5 ms transient.
- Add an inverting gain path around the op-amp referenced to `Vref`: use input resistor from `n3` to `Vinn` and feedback resistor from `Vout` to `Vinn`, with ratio `Rf/Rin > 29`, e.g. `Rin=10k`, `Rf=390k`.
- Include output limiting or finite-supply clipping so `Vout` remains within 0 V to 5 V and settles into a sustained nonlinear oscillation rather than numerical runaway. A behavioral source with limiting around the high-gain differential output is acceptable if no subcircuit is used.

Biasing assumptions:
- Single-supply operation requires all small-signal nodes to be biased around `Vref=2.5 V`.
- The non-inverting input `Vinp` should be fixed at `Vref`.
- The inverting input `Vinn` should sit near `Vref` at DC.
- If MOS transistors are used for the gain stage, connect NMOS bulk to source and PMOS bulk to source, use the provided `NMOS` and `PMOS` models, and bias gates/sources so devices exceed threshold. A behavioral op-amp/gain block is preferred for robust oscillator evaluation.
- Add startup asymmetry with `.ic` or initial capacitor/node conditions, for example `V(n1)=2.55`, `V(n2)=2.45`, `V(n3)=2.52`, or a small `Vinp/Vinn` mismatch. This is required because a perfectly symmetric oscillator can remain at the DC operating point.

Required analysis:
- Include exactly the requested transient analysis: `.tran 1u 5m`
- Save or expose `V(Vout)` as the primary waveform.

Expected behavior:
- At startup, the small initial mismatch around the op-amp input is amplified.
- The RC ladder selects the oscillation frequency and returns a phase-shifted signal to the inverting input.
- `Vout` should grow from near `Vref`, then settle into a sustained periodic waveform centered roughly around mid-supply.
- The latter half of the 5 ms transient should show clear nonzero standard deviation and visible oscillation amplitude at `Vout`, preferably hundreds of millivolts to several volts peak-to-peak, not a flat 20 mV ripple.
