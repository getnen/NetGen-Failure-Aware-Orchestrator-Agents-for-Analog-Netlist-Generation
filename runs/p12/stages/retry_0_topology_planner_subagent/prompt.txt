The filter should attenuate low frequencies, pass a midband region, and attenuate high frequencies, with a clearly defined center frequency visible in AC analysis.

Topology: use a cascaded passive RC band-pass structure:
1. A first-order high-pass section from `Vin` into an internal node.
2. A first-order low-pass section from that internal node to `Vout`.

Required devices:
- One independent AC voltage source: `V1 Vin 0 AC 1`
- One series capacitor for the high-pass input path
- One shunt resistor to ground for the high-pass section
- One series resistor for the low-pass section
- One shunt capacitor to ground at `Vout`

Recommended interconnectivity:
- Connect `V1` between `Vin` and `0`.
- Place capacitor `C1` from `Vin` to an internal node such as `N1`.
- Place resistor `R1` from `N1` to `0`.
- Place resistor `R2` from `N1` to `Vout`.
- Place capacitor `C2` from `Vout` to `0`.

Recommended values for a clear passband:
- `C1 = 10 nF`
- `R1 = 10 kOhm`
- `R2 = 10 kOhm`
- `C2 = 1 nF`

These values give:
- High-pass corner near `fL = 1 / (2*pi*R1*C1) ≈ 1.59 kHz`
- Low-pass corner near `fH = 1 / (2*pi*R2*C2) ≈ 15.9 kHz`
- Approximate band-pass center near `sqrt(fL*fH) ≈ 5.0 kHz`

Biasing assumptions: no DC supply rails are required because this is a passive small-signal AC filter. Ground node `0` is the only reference. No floating named inputs are allowed; `Vin` is explicitly driven by `V1`.

Operating expectation:

- In the midband, the high-pass section has passed the signal while the low-pass section has not yet rolled off strongly, so `Vout` reaches its maximum relative magnitude.
