**Topology Summary**

Single-supply inverting op amp differentiator on a 5 V rail. `Vin` is the triangle-wave input node, capacitively coupled through `C1` into the op amp inverting input node, e.g. `NINV`. `Vout` is the op amp output. The non-inverting input is tied to a mid-supply reference `Vref = 2.5 V`, so the circuit differentiates around `Vref` instead of ground.

Expected behavior:

`Vout ≈ Vref - Rf*C1*dVin/dt`

A rising ramp at `Vin` drives `Vout` below `Vref`; a falling ramp drives `Vout` above `Vref`, producing a square-like transient waveform limited by the 0 V and 5 V rails.

**Device/Function List**

- `VDD` independent DC source: 5 V supply from `VDD` to `0`.
- `Vin` independent voltage source: triangle waveform from `Vin` to `0`, centered around `Vref`.
- Vref divider: two equal resistors from `VDD` to `Vref` and from `Vref` to `0`.
- Op amp primitive/macromodel: powered from `VDD` and `0`, non-inverting input at `Vref`, inverting input at `NINV`, output at `Vout`.
- `C1`: from `Vin` to `NINV`, nominally 10 nF.
- `Rf`: from `Vout` to `NINV`, nominally 10 kOhm.
- Optional DC leakage resistor: very large resistor from `NINV` to `Vref` only for convergence, much larger than `Rf`.

**Bias/Reference Strategy**

Use `Vref = 2.5 V` as the signal common-mode reference. The op amp forces `NINV` to behave as a virtual `Vref` node during linear operation. The triangle source at `Vin` should be centered around 2.5 V with modest amplitude so the differentiated output remains mostly inside the 0 V to 5 V supply range.

**Signal Or Feedback Path**

Input current is generated by `C1` according to the slope of `Vin`. That current flows into the virtual-reference node `NINV` and is converted to output voltage through feedback resistor `Rf`. The feedback path is purely resistive from `Vout` to `NINV`, giving differentiator behavior with inversion.

**SPICE/Netlist Constraints**

- Final file should be `p25.cir`.
- Preserve node names exactly: `Vin`, `Vout`.
- `Vin` must be driven by an explicit independent triangle-wave voltage source.
- `Vout` must be the op amp output node.
- Include transient analysis: `.tran 1u 10m`.
- If using a behavioral op amp, its output must be limited between 0 V and 5 V.
- Stay within primitive-device/macromodel assumptions; do not replace the differentiator with a controlled-source shortcut unless implementing the op amp itself.

**Failure Risks To Avoid**

- Do not center `Vin` around 0 V; this breaks the single-supply bias assumption.
- Do not tie the non-inverting op amp input to ground; it must use `Vref`.
- Do not omit a DC path or convergence aid if the simulator struggles with the capacitive input node.
- Do not make the optional `NINV`-to-`Vref` resistor comparable to `Rf`; it would corrupt differentiator gain.
- Do not choose triangle slope, `Rf`, or `C1` so large that `Vout` is permanently railed unless visible clipping is intended.
- Do not swap polarity: rising `Vin` should produce lower `Vout`, falling `Vin` should produce higher `Vout`.
