**Topology Summary**

Use a flat, single-supply, stacked NMOS Gilbert cell mixer. The bottom NMOS RF differential pair converts `Vrfp - Vrfn` into differential currents at `rf_drp` and `rf_drn`. The upper NMOS LO switching quad steers those RF currents into either `Voutp` or `Voutn` depending on `Vlop - Vlon`. Two `10 kOhm` resistive loads from `VDD = 5 V` to `Voutp` and `Voutn` convert the commutated currents into a differential output voltage.

Expected behavior:

`Voutp - Voutn ∝ (Vrfp - Vrfn) × (Vlop - Vlon)`

With nominal 1 kHz RF and 1.2 kHz LO drives, transient analysis should show differential tones near `200 Hz` and `2.2 kHz`.

**Device/Function List**

- `Mtail`: NMOS tail current source
  - Drain: `rf_tail`
  - Source: `tail_src`
  - Gate: `Vbias_tail`
  - Bulk: `tail_src`
  - `tail_src` should be tied to ground or used as the grounded source node.

- `Mrfp`: RF positive input transconductor
  - Gate: `Vrfp`
  - Drain: `rf_drp`
  - Source/bulk: `rf_tail`

- `Mrfn`: RF negative input transconductor
  - Gate: `Vrfn`
  - Drain: `rf_drn`
  - Source/bulk: `rf_tail`

- `Mlo1`: LO switch steering `rf_drp` current to `Voutp`
  - Drain: `Voutp`
  - Source/bulk: `rf_drp`
  - Gate: `Vlop`

- `Mlo2`: LO switch steering `rf_drp` current to `Voutn`
  - Drain: `Voutn`
  - Source/bulk: `rf_drp`
  - Gate: `Vlon`

- `Mlo3`: LO switch steering `rf_drn` current to `Voutn`
  - Drain: `Voutn`
  - Source/bulk: `rf_drn`
  - Gate: `Vlop`

- `Mlo4`: LO switch steering `rf_drn` current to `Voutp`
  - Drain: `Voutp`
  - Source/bulk: `rf_drn`
  - Gate: `Vlon`

- `Rloadp`: `10 kOhm` from `VDD` to `Voutp`
- `Rloadn`: `10 kOhm` from `VDD` to `Voutn`

**Bias/Reference Strategy**

Use `VDD = 5 V`.

Bias the RF input sources around approximately `2.0 V` common mode with small opposite-phase 1 kHz signals. This keeps the RF pair on without overdriving the stacked devices.

Bias the LO inputs around a common mode high enough to switch the upper quad, likely around `3 V` to `4 V`, with opposite-phase 1.2 kHz signals. The LO amplitude should be large enough to alternate conduction between the cross-coupled switch pairs.

Set `Vbias_tail` around `1.1 V` to `1.3 V` for the given NMOS model:

`.model NMOS NMOS (VTO=0.7 KP=100u)`

This should create moderate tail current while avoiding output saturation. With `10 kOhm` loads, choose the tail current so `Voutp` and `Voutn` sit near mid-supply, not pinned to `5 V` or ground.

Tie every NMOS bulk to its local source node as specified.

**Signal or Feedback Path**

There is no stabilizing feedback loop. This is an open-loop switching transconductance mixer.

Signal flow:

`Vrfp - Vrfn` drives the lower differential pair, producing opposite drain currents at `rf_drp` and `rf_drn`.

`Vlop - Vlon` drives the upper quad, commutating those RF currents into `Voutp` and `Voutn`.

The resistive loads convert current steering into voltage swings. The cross-connected LO devices invert the output polarity on alternate LO phases, creating the multiplication action.

**SPICE/Netlist Constraints**

The final file should be named `p19.cir`.

The final netlist must be flat: no subcircuits, no behavioral multiplier, no hidden controlled-source shortcut.

Required exact external node names:

- Inputs: `Vrfp`, `Vrfn`, `Vlop`, `Vlon`
- Outputs: `Voutp`, `Voutn`

The netlist must include independent voltage sources explicitly connected to `Vrfp`, `Vrfn`, `Vlop`, and `Vlon`, even if the checker later replaces them.

Include:

- `VDD` source fixed at `5 V`
- `Vbias_tail` source
- Two `10 kOhm` loads
- NMOS model with `Vth/VTO = 0.7 V`, `KP = 100u`, `W = 50u`, `L = 1u`
- `.tran 1u 5m`

**Failure Risks To Avoid**

- Do not omit explicit RF/LO voltage sources; the checker expects the ports to exist.
- Do not rename ports. Preserve `Vrfp`, `Vrfn`, `Vlop`, `Vlon`, `Voutp`, `Voutn` exactly.
- Do not accidentally connect the LO quad in a non-crossed pattern; wrong commutation polarity will suppress the desired differential product.
- Do not let `Voutp` and `Voutn` rail at `VDD` from too little current or collapse near ground from too much tail current.
- Do not choose LO common mode too low; the upper NMOS switches need enough gate voltage above `rf_drp/rf_drn`.
- Do not leave NMOS bulks floating or tied globally to ground when the spec requires source-tied bulks.
- Do not use PMOS loads, active loads, subcircuits, or behavioral multiplication; the requested topology is resistively loaded NMOS Gilbert cell.
