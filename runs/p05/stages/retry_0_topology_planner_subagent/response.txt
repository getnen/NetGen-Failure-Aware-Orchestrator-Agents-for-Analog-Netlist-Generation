**Topology Summary**
Single-ended NMOS cascode common-source amplifier. Use two stacked NMOS devices from `Vout` down to ground, with `Rload` pulling `Vout` up to `VDD = 5 V`. The lower device converts `Vin` voltage changes into drain current; the upper cascode device shields the lower drain and raises output resistance, producing voltage gain at `Vout`.

**Device/Function List**
- `M1`: lower NMOS common-source input transistor.
  - Drain: internal cascode node, e.g. `Ncas`
  - Gate: `Vin`
  - Source: `0`
  - Bulk: `0`
  - Function: transconductance input device.

- `M2`: upper NMOS cascode transistor.
  - Drain: `Vout`
  - Gate: `Vbias`
  - Source: internal cascode node `Ncas`
  - Bulk: `Ncas`
  - Function: cascode device, increases output resistance and stabilizes M1 drain voltage.

- `Rload`: resistor from `VDD` to `Vout`.
  - Nominal value: `50k`
  - Acceptable range: `10k` to `100k`
  - Function: converts signal drain current into output voltage.

- `VDD`: independent DC source from `VDD` to `0`, value `5 V`.

- `Vin`: independent voltage source driving exact node `Vin`.
  - DC bias nominally `1.2 V`
  - AC magnitude `1`

- `Vbias`: independent voltage source driving exact node `Vbias`.
  - DC nominally `2.5 V`

**Bias/Reference Strategy**
Use the given NMOS model target: `Vth = 0.7 V`, `kp = 100u`, `W = 50u`, `L = 1u`.

`Vin = 1.2 V` gives M1 about `0.5 V` overdrive, enough for moderate conduction. `Vbias = 2.5 V` biases M2 on and should place the internal cascode node near a voltage that keeps M1 in saturation while preserving output swing at `Vout`. The `.op` point should confirm both NMOS devices conduct and remain in saturation with reasonable headroom below `VDD`.

**Signal Or Feedback Path**
There is no intentional global feedback loop. The small-signal path is:

`Vin` → M1 gate modulation → current through M1/M2 stack → voltage drop across `Rload` → inverted output at `Vout`.

M2 provides local cascode action by holding the M1 drain/internal node relatively stiff, reducing Miller effect and increasing gain.

**SPICE/Netlist Constraints**
- Preserve exact external node names: `Vin`, `Vbias`, `Vout`.
- `Vin` and `Vbias` must be driven by explicit independent voltage sources.
- `Vout` must be the drain of `M2` and the lower terminal of `Rload`.
- Use no subcircuits.
- Include `.op`.
- Include `.ac dec 20 1 1e9`.
- `Vin` source should include `AC 1` for direct gain measurement from `Vin` to `Vout`.
- Tie each NMOS bulk to its own source: M1 bulk to `0`, M2 bulk to internal cascode node.

**Failure Risks To Avoid**
- Do not leave `Vin` or `Vbias` floating or only referenced by comments/parameters.
- Do not tie M2 bulk to ground if the requirement is bulk tied to source.
- Do not place `Rload` from `Vout` to ground; it must connect from `VDD` to `Vout`.
- Do not accidentally make `Vout` the internal cascode node; `Vout` is the M2 drain.
- Avoid a bias point where `Rload` is too large or current too high, pulling `Vout` too low and forcing M2 out of saturation.
- Avoid setting `Vbias` too low, which can turn off M2 or starve M1 drain voltage.
- Avoid omitting `AC 1` on the `Vin` source, since gain analysis depends on it.
